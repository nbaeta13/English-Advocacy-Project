The system was working against you

You were not making irrational choices. You were making economically rational ones within a system that does not reward nutrition. A cheeseburger and fries delivers more calories per dining dollar than a grain bowl with vegetables, which makes it the logical choice in such a system.

Penn State's Student Farm already produces 16,000+ pounds of fresh vegetables per season. The infrastructure exists, but what's missing is a framework that routes it affordably to students who need it most.

Now meet the three proposed policies that could change this:

🌿
The Nittany Nutrition Pipeline
Student Farm and local-vendor produce designated and priced at cost-plus-5%, significantly cheaper than current market-rate items. Clearly labeled in the dining commons.
💳
The Healthy Choice Credit
A $25/month credit automatically loaded for Pell Grant and financial aid recipients, restricted to Nittany Nutrition items. No application needed.
🔄
Semester-End Rollover
Up to $75 in unused dining dollars rolls over semester-to-semester (restricted to Nittany Nutrition items). Currently, all unused funds are forfeited.
